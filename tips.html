Tips By Gudie ?

  1. - Choose your study source carefully. If you enjoy reading, go for books or articles, and so on.

  2. - Start by studying beginner-level sources first, then move to intermediate, and finally advanced ones.

  3. - Review your lessons regularly. Studies show that reviewing increases memory retention by 70%.

  4. - If you're following a course, don’t skip any lesson — each one builds on the previous.

  5. - Follow the 20-minute study method: study for 20 minutes, then take a 5-minute break. This gives your brain time to rest and helps store more information.

  6. - Create a study schedule to organize your time as much as possible
